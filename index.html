Getting to know INVESTOPEDIA

Yufei Jin (2019-03-17)

Whether you’re in a classroom, a boardroom or your living room, Investopedia editors and network of financial advisors and experts can help you understand complex financial concepts, improve your investing skills, and learn how to manage your money. Used properly, the Investopedia website can be quite effective in expanding your domain knowledge in investment.

Now let’s get to know Investopedia from its history, standards, editorial principles, and some of its features that I personally find most useful.

  • History

    ​

    Investopedia was founded by Cory Wagner and Cory Janssen in Edmonton, Alberta at the height of the dot-com era in 1999. Wagner focused on business development and R&D, and Janssen focused on marketing and sales. Investopedia drew about 2,500,000 monthly users and provided a popular financial dictionary with over 5,300 terms drawn from personal finance, banking and accounting. It also provided thousands of in-depth articles by financial experts and a Stock Simulator.

    In 2007, Forbes Media acquired Investopedia.com for an undisclosed amount.

    In 2010, Forbes sold Investopedia to ValueClick Inc. for $42 Million.

    In 2013, IAC/InterActive Corp acquired Investopedia.com as part of a group of internet properties from ValueClick Inc for $80 Million.

    In 2015, CEO David Siegel joined Investopedia.

    In June 2016, Investopedia launched Investopedia Academy[8] to sell premium video educational courses.

    In July 2018, Investopedia joined the Dotdash family of brands.

  • Standards

    ​

    Investopedia is committed to producing content and experiences that meet the highest journalistic and ethical standards. Much of the content is educational, and being accurate, transparent and insightful are the core principles. Investopedia is also committed to reporting on the people, companies, industries and economies that make up the ecosystem of the financial world in an unbiased and impartial manner. Its mission is to help educate the users so they can make better decisions for themselves, their families, their companies and their clients.

  • Editorial Principles

    ​

    Accuracy

    Investopedia presents accurate and dependable information in all forms whether that be analysis, education, advice or anything in between. The content is reviewed by multiple editors and experts to meet its high standards with particular attention paid to formulas, facts, figures and examples.

    Transparency

    Investopedia is very clear to the readers about its sources of information and the context in which the information was obtained. It frequently sources other publications, books, reports or other media in collecting the information presented to the readers, but, ultimately, the content is produced by the expert editors and writers of Investopedia, and is not crowd-sourced. Content that is created in collaboration with an advertiser will be clearly labeled as sponsored and presented as such. Investopedia takes full responsibility for its content.

    Insightful

    Investopedia strives to provide unique and valuable insights to the readers. By simplifying the complex and demystifying financial jargon Investopedia can educate its readers about finance and investing in a way that leads them to making smarter decisions.

  • Best Features

    ​

    Two of its features that I use most frequently are dictionary and video definitions .

    You can go to the dictionary to look up the financial terms with which you are not familiar. All the terms are arranged alphabetically.

    Some of the most important financial, business, and economic concepts have their own video definitions where the terms are thoroughly explained with detailed examples.
